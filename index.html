Modular Architecture

  1. Status Module

— Different tile faces indicate the landscapes that the playeres are supposed to choose cards from
— The level that a player has reacehd determines how far he haw progressed in the game
— A player's health status determines whether he/she can progress in the game

  2. Randomization Module

— Players randomly draw cards to progress in the game
— Throws a die or spins a wheel to progress in the game
— Randomize the position of the tiles to have a different map layout for every game

  3. Acheivement System

— Player with no health will not be able to move for certain rounds
— Player can only progress after answering questions correctly
— Each wrong decisions will have different comsequences

Design Rules

   1. Package Designer

1. Number and dimensions of the game pieces
2. Size of game board

  2. Graphic Designer

1. Size of box (for packaging artwork)
2. Number and dimensions of game pieces
3. Size of game board
4. Package dimensions
5. Card dimensions and appearance

  3. Logic Designer

1. Flow of the game
2. Consequences of different actions made by player

  4. Artifact Designer

1. Individual character tokens
2. Map tiles with various terrain and conditions
3. Scenario cards with questions and answers
4. Foldable health cards

Non-functional Requirements

1. The mechanics of game play (i.e. set-up and piece movement) shall not exceed 10 minutes with child players for 1 full round of gameplay
2. Rules of the game shall be understandable in ten minutes
3. There shall be twenty questions per game tile
4. There shall be at least 4 different game tiles depicting 4 different terrain/scenario
5. The game shall be available to play 24 hours per day, 365 days per year
6. The cost of the game shall be no more than $40
7. The board shall be sturdy enough to endure wear and tear in the event that rowdy children play it
8. There shall be no offensive language used in the game questions and answers
9. The game shall be able to played more than 15 times, still with new questions
10. The game shall be simple enough that children as young as 6 years old are able to understand immediately

Requirements Allocation

1. Packaging team materials costs shall not exceed $12
2. Graphic design team material costs shall not exceed $18
3. Artifact team material costs shall not exceed $10
4. Logic design shall not incur any monetary costs
5. Game setup by children only shall not require more than 10 minutes with 95% confidence
